The year is 2020, COVID-19 is widespread and running rampant in society.

Singapore has put in place measures known as the Circuit Breaker, where it's citizens have to adhere to social distancing measures responsibly.

You have been complying responsibly for the past month, but have been growing increasingly restless as you miss your friends and meeting up with them.

Temptation

You were minding your own business, watching YouTube videos and contemplating whether or not to finally cave in and attempt the latest Instagram stay-home trends when you see a notification on your phone.

"I've had enough of this, let's sneak out and meet up at a park to "exercise alone" together."

Your group chat immediately starts pinging with messages, and after some debating and polling, it seems that your friends have been bored out of their minds and either want to go out or have a Zoom session to catch up with everyone's lives. The deciding vote was down to you. Which choice do you make?

You cast the deciding vote and your friends accept that following the law is the smartest thing to do for everyone involved. The virus is not a joke. You boot up your laptop and hop onto a Zoom call with all your friends.

You decide to sneak out. You decide that the meeting point is the park near all of your houses. You put on your mask and make your way down to the park. 5 minutes after meeting everybody, your suspiciously large group is questioned by the police officers patrolling and you get detained.
